Process & Technical Documentation
System Migration Process Guide

A step-by-step process document outlining the workflow for migrating a legacy client management platform to a new enterprise system — designed for both technical teams and business operations staff.

MadCap Flare Process Mapping Technical Writing Cross-functional
Overview
This document was designed to support a multi-phase platform migration affecting 200+ users across three business units. The goal was to reduce support tickets by providing clear, role-specific guidance that non-technical users could follow independently.
Document Excerpt — Pre-Migration Checklist
  • Confirm data export from legacy system is complete and validated against record count
  • Notify all affected users of migration window (minimum 5 business days in advance)
  • Disable write-access to legacy system 24 hours prior to cutover
  • Verify that user accounts in new system are provisioned and tested by IT lead
  • Run parallel access test: confirm at least one user per role can log in successfully
  • Archive legacy system documentation in shared drive under /Archive/[ProjectName]/[Year]
  • Confirm rollback plan is documented and approved by project sponsor
Outcomes & Impact
Migration completed on schedule with zero data loss. Support ticket volume related to the migration dropped by 40% compared to a prior migration event, attributed to clearer pre- and post-migration documentation.

Onboarding Program Design
New Hire Onboarding Program: Enterprise SaaS

A 30-60-90 day onboarding program framework developed for a SaaS benefits platform, covering role-specific learning milestones, resource site architecture, and performance checkpoints.

Degreed Pathways Curriculum Design Stakeholder Alignment New Hire Experience
Program Overview
Designed for a 60-person customer operations team onboarding to two SaaS benefits platforms. The program replaced an ad-hoc shadowing model with a structured, measurable curriculum tied to role competencies and 90-day performance expectations.
Program Structure — 30 / 60 / 90 Day Milestones
  • Day 1–30 (Foundation): Platform navigation, core product knowledge, internal tools orientation, and compliance requirements. Delivered via Degreed Pathways + live facilitated sessions.
  • Day 31–60 (Application): Role-specific workflow training, case shadowing, and guided practice using real client scenarios (anonymized). QA tracking begins.
  • Day 61–90 (Independence): Solo case handling with manager check-ins, performance barometer review, and feedback loop with L&D to refine future cohorts.
Stakeholder Touchpoints
Program included bi-weekly reporting to department leadership with onboarding progress dashboards, and a post-90-day survey to measure learner confidence and identify content gaps.
Outcomes
Time-to-productivity reduced by approximately 3 weeks. Learner confidence scores (self-reported at 90 days) increased by 28% compared to the prior unstructured cohort.

Learning Pathways & Curriculum
Role-Based Upskilling Pathway: Operations Track

A curated multi-track learning pathway built in Degreed for an operations team, mapping LinkedIn Learning content, internal documentation, and custom modules to specific role levels and career growth goals.

Degreed LinkedIn Learning Career Development Content Curation
Design Rationale
Leadership identified a gap between onboarding completion and long-term engagement. This pathway was designed to give employees a clear, self-directed route for growth beyond the first 90 days — tied to defined competency levels and visible within their Degreed profile.
Track Structure (Operations Associate → Senior)
  • Foundation Track: Core systems fluency, communication skills, intro to data tools (LinkedIn Learning curated playlist, ~6 hrs)
  • Intermediate Track: Workflow optimization, cross-team collaboration, process documentation fundamentals (internal modules + LinkedIn Learning)
  • Advanced Track: Project leadership, coaching peers, contributing to SOPs, presenting to leadership
  • Stretch Goals: Optional modules in data analysis, change management, and business writing for high-performers
Curation Approach
Each module was reviewed against role competency definitions before inclusion. Third-party content (LinkedIn Learning) was supplemented with internal documentation to ensure platform-specific relevance. Pathway was reviewed quarterly and updated based on learner feedback and LOB changes.

Knowledge Management & Content Migration
Enterprise Knowledge Base Migration & Taxonomy Design

End-to-end project to audit, restructure, and migrate a fragmented internal knowledge base from multiple source systems into a unified Confluence architecture — including content lifecycle governance and a new taxonomy framework.

Confluence MadCap Flare Content Audit Information Architecture Jira
Project Scope
Inherited a knowledge base of 400+ documents spread across Google Drive folders, SharePoint sites, and a legacy intranet — with no consistent naming conventions, outdated content, and no owner accountability. Project goal: migrate all active content to Confluence with a sustainable governance model.
Migration Workflow
  • Phase 1 — Audit: Inventoried all existing content, flagged outdated/duplicate documents, and assigned preliminary content owners by LOB
  • Phase 2 — Taxonomy Design: Developed a three-tier taxonomy (Business Unit → Function → Document Type) with standardized naming conventions
  • Phase 3 — Migration: Migrated active content to Confluence in structured sprints tracked in Jira; retired 38% of audited content as outdated or redundant
  • Phase 4 — Governance: Established a quarterly content review cadence, content owner accountability framework, and a "last reviewed" metadata standard
Outcomes
Search success rate (users finding the right document on first attempt) increased by 52% post-migration based on a 30-day post-launch survey. Content owner accountability increased with 90% of documents assigned to a named owner with review dates set.

My Approach

01

02

03

04
